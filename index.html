The New Research Paradigm
Why Agentic Workflows?

Agentic workflows are emerging as a new paradigm for scientific discovery, in which AI systems do not merely answer questions or assist with isolated tasks, but actively participate in the research loop: proposing hypotheses, generating candidate solutions, writing and modifying code, running experiments or evaluations, learning from feedback, and iteratively improving their outputs. Recent systems such as AlphaEvolve have shown that large language models, when coupled with evolutionary search and rigorous evaluators, can discover new algorithms and optimizations that improve on existing state-of-the-art solutions in mathematics, computer science, and large-scale computing systems. More recent frameworks such as SkyDiscover and NanoEvolve extend this idea by making the discovery loop modular and open, enabling researchers to plug in their own problems, evaluators, search strategies, and domain-specific constraints. Together, these developments suggest that agentic scientific workflows may become a powerful new research methodology: one in which faculty and student teams bring deep domain expertise, while AI agents help explore large design spaces, automate experimentation, and uncover promising directions that may lead to new results and publications.

About the Workshop
What is AIDE?

AIDE is a collaborative workshop that helps faculty and student research teams explore how agentic AI workflows can accelerate scientific discovery. Participants bring their own research problem and work alongside AI-focused graduate student collaborators to implement and evaluate agentic approaches on real research challenges.

Through daily tutorials, implementation support, research sprints, and community discussions, AIDE provides the tools, infrastructure, and expertise needed to rapidly prototype agentic research workflows. The goal is simple: determine whether these emerging methods can help generate new insights, improve research productivity, and ultimately lead to publishable scientific advances.

How It Works

Research Teams

Faculty bring their own research problem and may be one or two graduate students from their research group to form a research team.

AI Collaborators

We will identify a few graduate students who will act as consultants/serious collaborators to help each research team run their problem with the agentic workflow to see if it can improve the state of the art. Hopefully, that leads to new results and publications.
